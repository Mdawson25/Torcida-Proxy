What is Torcida

Torcida is a cigar companion app built for everyone who loves cigars — whether you smoked your first one last weekend or you've been collecting for decades. We believe the cigar world is at its best when it's open, generous, and welcoming to all.

Inside Torcida you'll find an AI-powered cigar scanner to identify and learn about any cigar, a personal humidor tracker to manage your collection, a wish list for the ones you're hunting, a smoking journal to capture every memory, and a full reference guide covering everything from shapes and strengths to how to properly light a cigar for the first time.

Whether you need to know the difference between a Robusto and a Churchill, or you just want to remember that perfect smoke on a Tuesday evening in September — Torcida is built for you.

Cigar Culture is for Everyone

Cigar culture has a long and storied history, but it has not always opened its arms wide enough. Torcida was built on a simple belief: the ritual of a great cigar belongs to anyone who wants it.

You don't need to know the difference between a Habano and a Corojo wrapper to deserve a seat at the table. You don't need to have been smoking for twenty years. You don't need to speak a certain language, earn a certain income, or fit a certain image. All you need is curiosity and a willingness to slow down.

The cigar is one of the few rituals left in modern life that genuinely demands patience. It cannot be rushed. It rewards attention. And in that shared slowness, something remarkable happens — strangers become friends, and conversations go deeper. That is the culture we want to build around Torcida.

Why We Are Called Torcida

The name Torcida is a deliberate and heartfelt tribute to the people who make every premium cigar possible — the men and women whose skilled hands transform raw tobacco leaf into the cigars we love.

In Spanish, torcida is the feminine past participle of torcer — meaning something that has been carefully twisted and rolled by hand. It is what every premium cigar literally is. A thing made with patience, precision, and generational craft.

But we chose the feminine form intentionally. Because the cigar industry — particularly in Cuba, Nicaragua, Honduras, and the Dominican Republic — has always been built significantly on the labor, skill, and artistry of women. The escogedoras who sort and grade the leaves. The rezagadoras who select and match wrappers by color and texture. The torcedoras who roll cigars with a precision that takes years to master. Their names rarely appear on the bands. Their faces are rarely in the advertisements. But without them, there is no cigar.
